## Why this matters

The corporate structure is the legal-minimum form of a shell-style holding: €2,500 share capital, single owner-director, 2 employees, no VASP license despite handling Bitcoin payouts, repeated annual-report filing failures, current tax debt. This is consistent with an opaque payment business operating from a low-substance Estonian wrapper — legal under current Estonian law, but with no consumer-facing accountability if the operator chooses to stop honoring withdrawals.

Estonia's regulatory regime is changing: from **01 July 2026**, all VASP licenses issued by the FIU are revoked and any crypto-service provider must hold a new CASP license issued by the Estonian Financial Supervision Authority (FSA) under MiCA. CryptoCompany OÜ is not currently a VASP, but if Estonia or the FSA classify mining-payout operators as CASPs under MiCA, the company will need to comply — or relocate.
